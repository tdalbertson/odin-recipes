Pork Katsu

Description

One of Japan's specialties is Tonkatsu (とんかつ、豚カツ) or deep-fried pork cutlet.

Although it may seem challenging to make this Japanese staple, it is actually quite easy to prepare!

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Flour
  • Panko
  • Neutral flavored oil for deep frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • White & Black Sesame seeds (optional)
  • Shredded Cabbage (optional)

Instructions

Preparing the Shredded Cabbage (Optional)

  1. You can cut the cabbage extremely thinly with a sharp knife or use a cabbage slicer.

Making the Sesame Tonkatsu Sauce (Optional)

  1. Grind the white and black toasted sesame seeds with a mortal and pestle. Leave some seeds unground for texture.
  2. In individual small plates or bowls, serve 1 Tbsp of the ground sesame seeds and add tonkatsu sauce - mix together when ready to eat.

Making Fresh Panko

  1. Put store-bought dry panko in a deep dish and spray with water until the panko is moist. Set aside for 15 minutes, or until the panko becomes soft and tender.

Preparing the Pork

  1. With a sharp knife, remove the extra fat and make several slits on the connective tissue between the meat and the fat.
  2. Flip the meat and make several slits on the other side of the connective tissue.
  3. Pound both sides of the meat with the back of a knife or a meat tenderizer.
  4. Mold the meat back into its original shape with your hands.
  5. Season both sides of the meat with salt and pepper.
